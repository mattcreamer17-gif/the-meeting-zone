The frame

60 / 30 / 10 applied to a 20,000 person matching problem.

Most matchmaking problems at a 20,000 person conference are not AI problems. Sixty percent is clean data: passholder profiles, meeting slots, physical zone capacity. Thirty percent is rule based logic: who is a sponsor, who is a buyer, who is an investor, who is a founder, what is the priority ordering. Ten percent is genuine AI: ranking candidate matches by intent, surfacing the non obvious pairing, learning from no shows. Skip the first two layers and the AI layer has nothing to stand on.

60%

Clean data

Passholder profiles, meeting slots, physical zone capacity. Traditional code and database work, not an LLM problem.

30%

Rule based logic

Sponsor tiers, buyer and investor flags, founder tags, priority ordering. Deterministic rules, not model inference.

10%

Genuine AI

Ranking candidate matches by intent, surfacing the non obvious pairing, learning from no shows. The part that actually needs a model.
